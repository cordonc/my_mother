Dear Paul,

We all miss you very much.

You wonder why I didn’t answer your question on the day you were leaving. The truth was that I was feeling quite sorry that day, and I didn’t know how ot answer it directly. Well, now I can tell you.

(1) Though Ting is married off to a place far away, I don’t feel worried at all because she has a good husband to take care of her.

(2) I feel a little sorry, indeed, but I am quite happy I know both of you love each other deeply. And that’s the basis of building a good family. What I concern most is whether you can live harmoniously instead of the place where Ting gets married. Suppose Ting is married in Taiwan, and her spouse doesn’t treat her well. Ting is the most important, not the place Ting is married to.

Congratulations in your marriage. By the way, some students want to say “Hello” to you. They all miss you, especially May and Cindy.

With love

Yei Yei (My Mothers Father)

my mother

Most of my conversations with my mom still circle back to “I should have never married your father.” It seems she is still reminiscing on the past, what could have been, the mistakes that were made, and how to move forward from it.

Pieces of her story are fed to me in passing conversation over the span of 10 years. My fathers wedding ring stationed in a cluttered drawer for years because ‘men don’t wear jewelry’. A couch that my father has slept on for 20 years because he prefers it over the master bedroom. My fathers absence in bed was replaced by us, her children, where she slept with us until we were old enough to sleep in our own bedrooms.

҉

I wonder how she sees herself. The pressure to be Americanized by her family– the idea of success pushed upon her to learn another language and adopt to someon else's cultural ideals. Only for a white man to fetishize her race and correct her English.

He speaks your language, but will never understand you.

She spent hours tutoring her native tongue to him, in an effort to feel seen and heard– only to be spat on with words of contempt.

The physical bruises were the necessary evidence to prove how much he bruised her inside.

Papers and court documents were gathered to present her case.

I wanted to find them.

I wanted to see them for myself.

The thick, heavy family photo books that line the shelves in her household hold no weight to this one court document.

I still can’t find them.
